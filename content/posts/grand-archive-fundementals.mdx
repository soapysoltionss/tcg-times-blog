# Grand Archive fundamentals

Side note: This is meant for players looking to improve their gameplay competitively. For beginners who just started out their games, I recommend checking out some videos on YouTube. 

## Fundamental 1: Choosing a Deck

Your next big competition is coming up in a few months, and you are contemplating if you should take that deck that you have been practicing diligently for the past few months, or if you should switch to the rising star deck that has been making waves in the meta.
This is part and parcel of every player’s journey in the competitive scene, and it is a question that has no right or wrong answer. It is a question that only you can answer for yourself, and it is a question that you will have to answer multiple times throughout your competitive career.
However, there are some guiding principles that you can use to help you make this decision.

### Guiding Principle 1: Play to Your Strengths
The first guiding principle is to play to your strengths. If you are a player who is comfortable with a certain playstyle, then it is generally a good idea to stick with that playstyle. 
For example, if you are a player who is comfortable with making proactive plays and taking risks, then it might be a good idea to stick with aggressive decks, such as suited allies or fire aggro. 
If you are a player who is comfortable with sitting back and watching your opponent suffer, then it might be a good idea to stick with control decks, such as luxem zander or astra arissana.

### Guiding Principle 2: Deck Archetypes - How to Identify Them
The second guiding principle is to understand deck archetypes and how to identify them. Deck archetypes are categories that describe the overall strategy and playstyle of a deck. Common archetypes include aggro, control, midrange, and combo. Recognizing the archetype of your deck and your opponent's deck can help you make better strategic decisions during a match.
