# Flesh and Blood Equipment Choices: How to Gear Up for Success

If you're coming to Flesh and Blood from another TCG, the equipment system is probably one of the first things that blew your mind. The ability to build your hero's *outfit* as a key part of your strategy is unlike anything in Magic, Pokémon, or most other card games.

But with great power comes great complexity. Let's break down how to think about equipment selection properly.

## The Four Slots

Every hero has access to four equipment slots:

- **Head** — Usually defensive or utility
- **Chest** — Often the most impactful slot; many heroes have defining chestpieces
- **Arms** — Frequently offers defense or a conditional ability
- **Legs** — Often generates resources or tokens

Understanding that each slot serves a different *function* in your gameplan is the first step to building intelligently.

## Defensive vs. Offensive Equipment

The core tension in equipment selection is **defense vs. offense**. Some equipment is purely defensive (block 2, break), while other pieces give you proactive value at the cost of defense.

A piece that blocks for 2 and breaks sounds boring — but consider:

- Against aggressive heroes, those 2 defense points might save your life
- Break effects can snowball your own turns
- Sometimes the "boring" equipment is the correct metagame call

### A Framework for Evaluation

When evaluating any equipment piece, ask:

1. **What is the floor?** — What's the worst this card does for me in a game?
2. **What is the ceiling?** — What's the best case scenario?
3. **How often do I reach the ceiling?** — Conditional abilities that rarely trigger are often overrated by new players

## Hero-Specific Considerations

Each hero has a "core" equipment package that maximizes their kit, but you can often deviate based on:

- **Your local meta** — If aggressive heroes dominate, bias toward defensive slots
- **Your playstyle** — If you prefer longer games, defensive equipment extends your clock
- **Your card pool** — Sometimes the "optimal" equipment piece isn't accessible to newer players

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Over-weighting Rare Equipment

Rarity doesn't equal power in FaB. Many of the strongest equipment pieces in Constructed are commons and uncommons. Don't assume a shiny Legendary chest is the correct choice.

### Mistake 2: Building Equipment Before Understanding Your Hand

Your equipment should *complement* your hand, not replace it. If you're constantly using equipment because your hand is weak, the problem might be your pitch curve, not your equipment suite.

### Mistake 3: Ignoring the Matchup

Equipment selection in a tournament setting often means picking a *configuration* for an expected metagame. One slot might shift based entirely on whether you expect to face aggro or control.

## Closing Thoughts

Equipment in Flesh and Blood is a meta-game within the meta-game. It's one of the format's greatest strengths — the ability to fine-tune your hero down to the literal clothes on their back.

Start simple, understand the functions of each slot, and iterate based on what you're seeing at your local events.
